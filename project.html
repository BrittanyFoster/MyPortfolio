FLYt Case Study

Summary

Cloud organization and storage is on the rise and there is unlimited potential for what it can become. Google Drive and Drop Box are some of the leading companies but there are still unexplored areas cloud storage can thrive. FLYt targets users on the go who share and store a lot of information quickly. Users can save, organize, and create content, upload files, share items and connect with other users for real time collaboration. In a rapidly changing world, FLYt is taking off to new possibilities.

Invision Prototype
Problem

The client initially mentioned that they see potential in cloud storage services to be an enhanced version of what already exists.



  1. 1. Staying connected
  2. 2. Expanding offline opportunities
  3. 3. Enhancing free services
  4. 4. Simplifying ways to access information
  5. 5. Resetting Password
  6. 6. Trusting in the cloud to store personal information


Overall, the client believes there are a lot of interesting and compelling features they could put into this new application, but does not yet have a clear vision, including a name or brand. The client isn’t sure if this could be a web app for desktop browsers, usable on mobile devices or both. They don’t have a target audience in mind and need a designer’s thoughts on the opportunity that plays to core strengths and brings it to the market.

Solution

FLYt simplifies the process of saving, creating, and organizing content into a platform that is easy for any user to navigate.

  1. 1. As many technologies are integrating, the user wants to feel that platforms are all connected, without having to exit out of one platform to get to another. There are currently limitations sharing via social media platforms, but with FLYt users will now stay connected at all times.
  2. 2. One of user’s biggest pain points is not being able to work offline when on the go. FLYt will allow the user to work offline and temporarily save content until back online.
  3. 3. An area of opportunity is giving more free storage for users, especially for college students and those just starting their career. FLYt’s solution is to start users out with 20 GB before upgrading to the next package.
  4. 4. FLYt users can access their stored information from any device in any format.
  5. 5. The process of resetting a password is tedious and instead of just providing the user with the original password, they are usually required to change it. With FLYt’s improved process, the user can retrieve their information quickly.
  6. 6. FLYt’s platform allows users to trust their information will be safe.

Competitive Analysis
Google Drive

Strengths

  • high standard for user security
  • access content easily from work or home from any device
  • user friendly and has a great platform for collaboration.

Weaknesses

  • editing difficulties with too many users
  • need to enhance plug in capabilities
  • threat of data loss and theft
Drop Box

Strengths

  • allowing users to control access privileges to share information securely
  • using a simple link to share a document and mobile accessibility
  • making the site convenient and easy for new users

Weaknesses

  • files not being synchronously edited
  • higher package pricing options
  • allowing IT control or visibility at business organizations
User Personas

64.7% of the users surveyed use the site for both personal and business needs and their feedback provided insight into what a user actually wants with a cloud storage service. I focused on three types of users: a college student and two working professionals, a man and a woman who are balancing personal and business life. For example, a Sales Consultant who travels a significant amount of time for the job needs a quick and reliable way to connect with his team while on the go.

User Stories

At first there were too many stories as high priority, but not all users would need features like sharing items and real time collaboration. Many users just want the basics of cloud storage so this divided the low, medium, and high priority tasks into three separate packages.

  1. I want to be able to:
  2. -create content, notes, and spreadsheets
  3. -easily share items with other people
  4. -upload photos and videos
  5. -store a bulk of content
  6. -have ample space to store documents

User Flows

At first I had every process mapped out but then simplified it to get the user from the landing page to the dashboard, then focus on all of the paths within the dashboard. Simplifying made it easier for me to understand how the site would be laid out and I was able to map out the clickable prototype based on the user flow.

Wireframes

Once creating the wireframes, it was interesting to see how people reacted when they walked through the steps. I could see where they were getting stuck and where the page needed to be enhanced for the real prototype.

The black and white image above was the original wireframe design of the site. The nav bar was one of the pieces that changed the most. I removed the Search Bar and added Sign In and FLYt Plans on the right. I also used the bird logo on the left to open a menu with Contact FLYt and Sign Up. The design at the end looked cleaner and more professional. Also all buttons needed to click toward something to solve the user’s needs. It needed to be easy to use, some steps being eliminated and some added.

Mockups

My original background screens were too colorful which overpowered the text and information on the page. Once I applied professional visuals, the creative design started to come together. I used icons from Sketch App Sources and visuals from Unsplash.com to enhance the appearance of the page. I enhanced the package features section with a grid and added details like rounding the edges of rectangles and was deliberate with the size, style, and amount of text.


I added professional images to break up the text information and improve the quality of the design. Then, I took ideas from existing cloud services to come up with a platform of my own. I wanted all users to be able to easily navigate the site and but also have the feel of a top of the line, elite platform. The foundation of the dashboard design was based on Google Drive. I took what I liked and added my own design to give the site its own distinct look and feel.


I wanted to add colors into the design without it being overwhelming. I am also a huge fan of themes and I liked using the Flight theme throughout the site. Terms such as FLYt Deck, FLYt Storage, Lounge Access, and names for types of documents like FLYt Demo and FLYt Grid enhanced the brand and theme and brought it altogether.

Branding Work

I developed an idea of using a hummingbird since they move so fast and effortlessly in the air. It seemed to be a perfect analogy for a cloud service. Because the bird was looking down, it appeared to be looking at the information beneath it. Also being an outline of a bird, I could fill in the logo with one color. The final logo looked sharp and sophisticated.


I used a bright, bluish purple with a 20% opacity for the navigation bar. Simplifying the website colors allowed for a variety of other colors to pop out, like a champagne gold in the background images. The name Flyt came to me pretty quickly, but then I capitalized more letters to create FLYt with a focus on the word FLY. Adding the last letter as a lower case became a stylistic choice used on other text throughout the site.

Prototypes (with Iterations)

Once I used professional images, I was able to create a classy backdrop for the landing page. The new design had a more professional layout and the colors didn’t overwhelm the page. I removed unnecessary titles and aligned the icons and text until the display looked perfect. Once I found the right combination of pictures, icons, text, and creativity, the site really came together.


User Feedback

  • Using visuals or white for the background
  • Putting titles in the features section
  • Creating a grid for the Package Options section
  • Changing the names of the Packages
  • Adding a Trash section in the left tools bar
  • Adding the Share Button to all documents
  • Adding icons to the docs, presentations, etc.
  • Curving the edges of the documents
  • Removing titles that overcrowded the page
  • Fixing the language in customer feedback sections
  • Adding visuals that shows the app in use
  • Fixing the nav bar to stay in position

Final Design

For the first two frames, I added sharp background images that gave the site a professional feel. I modeled the design after Google Drive that broke up textual information with action images. The photos capture the app in use and paint a picture for the user.

The features page was tough to design because the icons in Sketch App Sources were very finicky and were difficult to align perfectly with the text. I put the information in boxes so it stood out from the background in four boxes straight across a row. The information is now easier for the reader to digest and allows them to click to the next page to learn more about that particular feature.

A few users suggested that I organize the package information in the form of a grid to make it easier to understand what each package offers. I had a variety of colors for the different packages, but in the end used one solid purple for simplicity.

On the final page, I changed the background image to match the first image with white and champagne gold accents. I aligned everything so the information was organized and didn’t overwhelm the page.



Now that the theme of the website is developing, I can get a lot more creative and make the site something unique for a Cloud Organization and Storage service.

Conclusion



Receiving user feedback was the most helpful tool in improving my design. Each person offered new insight and I usually agreed with what they were saying. I learned that giving images and text more space to breathe is one of the most significant aspects of web design. The background should support the information, not overwhelm it. Simplifying colors, text, images, and spacing brought it altogether.

What did not work was trying to guess what the website design should look like and relying solely on my own knowledge to build it. I knew it didn’t look great at first but also didn’t have the tools like Unsplash.com and Sketch App sources to implement images.

I learned that I have a lot of the skills to be a UX Designer, it was just a matter of putting it into a new format. I have a lot of creative ideas and I know I can do even better in the next project.

In the end, I was highly pleased with how the site turned out. I thought it not only met the client’s initial expectations, but gave them a unique creative brand and enticing visual design. It focused on fixing the customer's pain points and I believe the client would be satisfied with the end result and will now be able to move forward to launch their cloud platform.
