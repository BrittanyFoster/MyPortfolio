LimeLite Case Study

Summary

LimeLite is a job marketing platform that targets ambitious applicants who desire to stand out from their competition. Users can create, upload, and save resumes, cover letters, portfolios, and other content. In a challenging job market, LimeLite will highlight specific applicant qualities, connect them with employers, and help them land their dream job.

  Figma Prototype
Problem

The client believes it's difficult for applicants to stand out from the competition, get their resume into the right hands, and convey their real abilities through an online application.



  1. 1. Uploading files
  2. 2. Creating and editing content
  3. 3. Searching for job listings
  4. 4. Posting job listings
  5. 5. Connecting with colleagues and recruiters
  6. 6. Discovering additional methods to stand out above the competition


Overall, the client believes a huge advancement can be made in online employment and applicants today are at a disadvantage in finding their ideal job. They need a designer's insight of who to market to and how to execute the product.

Solution

LimeLite resolves the client's initial problems by creating a dashboard design with four different layouts for features.

  1. The user can:
  2. 1. Upload files on the Sharpen My Tools section.
  3. 2. Create an 'edit content' option in the Sharpen My Tools section.
  4. 3. Search for and post jobs in the Search & Apply section.
  5. 4. Connect with colleagues and recruiters in the Connect section.
  6. 5. Showcase their personalized profile on the My Profile section.

Competitive Analysis
LinkedIn

Strengths

  • - most working people and businesses are using this site
  • - superb performance in new job markets

Weaknesses

  • - not all companies will list their available positions on the site

Indeed

Strengths

  • - skyrocketed in popularity in the past few years
  • - streamlines process by eliminating the need to visit dozens of sites to find relevant opportunities

Weaknesses

User Personas

There are multiple types of people to target, both employers and employees who have ideas of how to connect for a job. I focused on three types of professionals: a post college student, an HR recruiter, and a business owner. Getting ideas from both the applicant and employer will tighten the bond and connect like minded individuals quicker.

User Stories

The majority of users want the basic functions of job marketing, such as being able to keep information on file and being able to quickly apply for jobs. In addition, some want features that will make them stand out to potential employers such as uploading a video, creating an enhanced resume, and connecting with them at job marketing events.

  1. The user wants to be able to:
  2. -speed up the job application process
  3. -update resume, cover letter, and application
  4. -increase chances of getting an interview
  5. -upload videos, photos, and content to my profile
  6. -connect with potential employers

User Flows

User Flows posed a challenge to envision all scenarios of potential paths a user could take that was not the one intended. I created user flows based on high priority users, and thought of each scenario in which a user could get stuck getting to their destination.

Wireframes

When creating the design of the four dashboard pages, I wanted to be consistent, but also make each oage uniquely its own. While designing the page layouts, I envisioned what the users would want to see on the page. I kept it simple with the features as visual as possible to make the dashboard easy to navigate.

Mockups

Using a strong Hero image conveyed action. Originally, I used a desktop image which wasn't interesting enough, then a lighted background which didn't display any action. The Hero image I settled on shows applicants searching for a job while the second image shows the action of a person driving to and from work. Both convey action and growth from using the product without being too literal.


I added professional images to break up the text information and improve the quality of the design. Then, I took ideas from existing cloud services to come up with a platform of my own. I wanted all users to be able to easily navigate the site and but also have the feel of a top of the line, elite platform. The foundation of the dashboard design was based on Google Drive. I took what I liked and added my own design to give the site its own distinct look and feel.


I used a grid to make each element the exact same spacing. The end result appeared much cleaner and I added drop shadows to make boxes and images float on the pages.

Branding Work

Developing a unique logo and a brand was a challenge. At first I went too literal with the idea of networking and connecting with pretty basic logo designs. My creativity was lacking because the ideas were too literal and the brand develoment was not coming along.


A user suggested I get away from literal ideas and come up with abstract ones like Apple computers using an Apple logo. I started developing fruit logos and came up with LimeLite, which was a short lived idea I had for a previous project. The logo idea and branding matched the message of the company, which is all about getting yourself seen in the employment field. Once I had this "fruitful" idea, the creative juices started to flow.


The brand is geared more toward the younger generation. Since LinkedIn has a more professional adult vibe, I wanted to go a different route. The bright colors and young vibe of the logo matched with the intended audience of the younger generation. The brand can still be utilized by older generations, but has a light hearted, comtemporary vibe to intrigue the up and coming one.

Conclusion



The idea behind LimeLite started with a challenge of applicants to be seen in the working world, especially when transitioning into a new field. Most employers want to see several years of experience in the field, and few are willing to give an individual with no experience a chance. With this innovative platform, an applicant can highlight their skills, proactively reach out to employers, and make a strong impression before sitting down for an interview.

LinkedIn was created with a similar idea, to make connections and network in the workforce especially when most applicants are online.

Creating a colorful design and concept brought the site to life. The idea and brand became distinctly different and marketable.

Being on a time crunch set my mind into overdrive and I was able to complete each step of the process quickly. The more projects I complete, I realize I am not running short on creative ideas. The idea of LimeLite didn't come together for the last project but I was able to bring that idea into this project with a better platform.
